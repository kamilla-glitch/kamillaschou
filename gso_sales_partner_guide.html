📋 The Perfect DSR — Your Pre-Submit Checklist

Tick every item before submitting your DSR. Missing items = delays for your seller.

DSR Readiness Checklist

0/7
items complete
✓
Menu is attached and ready CRITICAL
Attach ANY menu — DoorDash link, website URL, CSV, or PDF. Do not submit a draft or partial menu. A menu that isn't confirmed with the seller = significant delays and disruptions. If the menu isn't ready, don't submit yet.
✓
Hardware is ordered + order link attached in DSR CRITICAL
Link the hardware order in the DSR notes. If the seller is reusing any hardware, clearly state this. GSO needs to know what's arriving and when — it directly affects the implementation timeline.
✓
Card processing is activated on the account CRITICAL
The seller must have card processing activated before GSO can begin. This is a hard blocker — check before submitting.
✓
Regulator link is attached
Attach the seller's Regulator link in the DSR. GSO uses this for billing, migrations, and account configuration.
✓
Detailed deal context notes included
Include: number of locations, current hardware setup, integrations, vertical/segment, complexity, any known Seller sensitivities or special requirements. The more context, the faster GSO can move. 💡 Use Revenue.io or ChatGPT to condense your notes.
✓
Seller is ready to kick off in the next 2–4 weeks
Do not submit if the seller won't be ready for 4+ weeks. GSO will reject the DSR and ask you to resubmit when the seller is ready. Submit only when there's genuine momentum.
✓
Introduction email sent to seller (or ready to send)
Once the DSR is submitted, send the seller intro email to GSO using the GSO Intro Email templates on Highspot. GSO will respond within 24 hours.

⚠️ The Top 3 Things That Slow Down Your Seller

These are the most common causes of delayed go-lives. All preventable at the DSR stage.

🍽️
#1 — No Menu (or Draft Menu)
Can add 3–7+ days of delay
The menu is the foundation of the implementation. Without a confirmed menu, GSO can't set up the catalog, which blocks every downstream training session. A draft or unconfirmed menu is just as bad as no menu.
✅ Fix: Attach any accepted menu format (DoorDash link, website URL, CSV, PDF). Confirm with the seller it's accurate before submitting.
📦
#2 — Hardware Not Ordered
Can delay go-live by weeks
If hardware isn't ordered by the time GSO starts, the seller can't go live — even if all the training is complete. Hardware delivery times are out of GSO's control, and late orders destroy go-live dates.
✅ Fix: Order hardware before submitting the DSR. Link the order in DSR notes. Flag any reused hardware clearly.
📝
#3 — Incomplete or Missing Notes
Costs time on the kickoff call
When GSO receives a DSR with sparse notes, we spend the kickoff call gathering information that should have been provided upfront. This adds friction to the seller's first interaction and burns implementation time.
✅ Fix: Use Revenue.io or ChatGPT to summarise deal context. Include locations, hardware, vertical, integrations, and anything unusual.
🚦 DSRs may be rejected if: menu is not attached, card processing isn't activated, the seller won't kick off within 2–4 weeks, or critical information is missing. We'll tell you why and what to fix.

🚀 How to Engage GSO — Step by Step
1
Complete the checklist above → File a DSR in Salesforce
All deals file through a Deal Support Request. For sub $10M sellers, standard DSR. For $10M+ (AU), still a standard DSR — see go/gsoroe for routing guidance. For Franchise Suite, file via SFF workflow.
2
Send the seller introduction email
Use the GSO Intro Email templates on Highspot. This introduces the seller to the GSO team and sets expectations. Add yourself as a required attendee on the kickoff call — you have a vested interest in it moving quickly.
3
GSO responds within 24 hours
Once assigned, GSO will send the seller a booking link for the kickoff call within 24 hours. If they haven't responded after 24 hours, give them a nudge via Chatter on the DSR.
4
Kickoff call happens — GSO takes over
After the kickoff call, GSO owns the seller's onboarding journey. You'll be cc'd on all seller emails and can track progress via DSR Chatter. GSO will tag you in Chatter with updates, action items, and blockers.
5
Your ongoing role: stay available for escalations
Watch Chatter for action items (e.g. hardware still hasn't arrived, seller is non-responsive). If GSO flags a blocker to you, act on it promptly — every day of delay is a day further from go-live.
6
Implementation Complete → Seller is live 🎉
GSO sends the final email and transitions the seller to Customer Success or AM. At this point, GSO disengages — redirect any future seller questions to CS or the appropriate AM channel.
📊 Tracking your seller's progress? All status updates happen in Salesforce DSR Chatter. GSO updates status after every seller interaction. You can see current status, go-live date, and next steps at any time.
